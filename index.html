Why prep matters

The floor that lasts is the one that's ground, not just rolled

Most coating failures — peeling, bubbling, hot-tire pickup — trace back to skipped prep, not the product. A coating rolled straight onto a sealed or dusty slab has nothing to grip.

We mechanically grind the concrete to open the surface, repair cracks and pits, and only then apply your chosen system. It's the difference between a floor that looks great for a season and one that's still solid years later.

  • Diamond grinding for a real mechanical bond
  • Crack, pit, and control-joint repair before coating
  • Moisture-aware system selection for NC humidity
  • Clean edges, consistent broadcast, even top coat
  • Clear walkthrough of finish and cure times up front

Common questions

Epoxy & garage floor coating FAQs

How much does a garage floor coating cost in Apex?

Coatings typically run about $3–$7 per square foot depending on the condition of your slab, how much crack and pit repair is needed, and the finish you choose. Metallic and multi-coat flake systems sit toward the higher end. Every slab is different, so the best number comes from a free on-site estimate.

How long does it take, and when can I park on it?

Most residential garages are a one- to two-day job. Polyaspartic top coats cure fast and can often handle foot traffic the next day, with vehicle traffic shortly after. Traditional epoxy systems need a little more cure time. We'll give you exact timing for your specific finish.

What's the difference between epoxy, flake, metallic, and polyaspartic?

Epoxy is the coating chemistry. Flake and metallic describe the look — flake uses decorative chips for texture and grip, metallic creates a marbled, poured appearance. Polyaspartic is a fast-curing, UV-stable top coat often used over flake systems for durability and quick return to service.

Will it peel or lift under hot tires?

Hot-tire pickup happens when a coating wasn't bonded properly — usually a paint or roll-on product over unprepped concrete. Professionally ground and coated floors with the right top coat are built specifically to resist it.

Do you coat basements and commercial floors too?

Yes. The same systems work for basement slabs, shops, warehouses, and light commercial spaces. Tell us about the space when you request an estimate and we'll recommend the right finish.
