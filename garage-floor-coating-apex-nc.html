A garage floor coating built for how you actually use the space

Your garage floor takes more abuse than almost any surface in your home — vehicle traffic, hot tires pulling in off Apex roads in July, oil drips, road salt in winter, and whatever you drop while working. A quality coating seals the concrete so spills wipe up instead of soaking in, brightens the whole space, and adds grip underfoot.

The finish is the part you see, but the prep is the part that makes it last. We diamond-grind the slab to open the concrete, fill cracks and pits, and address control joints before any coating goes down. That mechanical bond is what separates a professional coating from a roll-on garage paint that peels within a season.

Coating options for Apex garages

  • Full-flake systems — decorative vinyl chips broadcast into the base coat for texture, grip, and a finish that hides dust and minor slab imperfections.
  • Solid-color epoxy — a clean, uniform look in the color you choose, great for a bright, showroom-style garage.
  • Polyaspartic top coats — fast-curing and UV-stable, often walk-ready the next day, so your garage isn't out of use for long.

What you can expect

Most two- and three-car garages are a one- to two-day project. We start with a walkthrough and written estimate, grind and repair the slab, apply your base and finish coats, then seal it with a protective top coat. Before we leave, you'll know exactly when to walk on it and when to park on it.

Coatings in this area typically run about $3–$7 per square foot depending on slab condition, repair needs, and finish. Every garage is a little different, so we give you a firm number after seeing the floor — never a guess over the phone.

Common questions

Garage floor coating FAQs

How much does a garage floor coating cost in Apex, NC?

Plan on roughly $3–$7 per square foot. A standard two-car garage with a slab in decent shape falls toward the lower-to-middle of that range; heavy crack repair or premium metallic finishes push it higher. A free on-site estimate gives you an exact figure.

How long before I can park on it?

With polyaspartic systems, foot traffic is often fine the next day and vehicle traffic shortly after. Traditional epoxy needs a bit more cure time. We give you exact timing for the system you choose.

Can you coat over an old, peeling floor?

Usually yes — but the old coating and any loose material has to come off first. That's exactly what the grinding step handles, so the new system bonds to sound concrete rather than failing paint.

Is it slippery when wet?

Flake systems add natural texture, and we can add an anti-slip additive to the top coat for extra grip in garages, entries, and ramps.
